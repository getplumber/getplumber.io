This privacy policy sets out how we handle information when you visit www.r2devops.io. This is a frontend-only website with no user accounts, login functionality, or personal data storage.

## Information We Collect

**We do not collect or store personal information.** This website is a static frontend site with no backend database or user accounts. We do not require you to create an account or provide personal information to browse our website.

### Website Analytics

We use Plausible Analytics, a privacy-focused web analytics service, to understand how visitors interact with our website. Plausible Analytics:

- Does not use cookies
- Does not collect any personal data or personally identifiable information
- Does not track users across websites
- Complies with GDPR, CCPA, and PECR
- Provides aggregated statistics about website usage (page views, referrers, device types, etc.)

All analytics data is processed in aggregate form and cannot be used to identify individual visitors. For more information about Plausible Analytics' privacy practices, please visit [Plausible Analytics Privacy Policy](https://plausible.io/privacy).

### Contact Forms

If you choose to contact us through our contact form, we will receive the information you provide (such as your name, email address, and message). This information is used solely to respond to your inquiry and is not stored on our servers or used for any other purpose. We do not maintain a database of contact form submissions.

### Newsletter Signup

If you choose to subscribe to our newsletter, your email address will be processed by our newsletter service provider. We do not store newsletter subscriber information on our servers. Please refer to our newsletter provider's privacy policy for information about how they handle your data.

## Cookies and Consent

Browsing the www.r2devops.io site may cause the installation of cookie(s) on the user's computer. A cookie is a small file, which does not allow the identification of the user, but which records information relating to the navigation of a computer on a site. The data thus obtained are intended to facilitate subsequent navigation of the site, and are also intended to allow various measures of attendance. Those data will allow the accounting of interactions on articles (comments and likes). Refusal to install a cookie may make it impossible to access certain services. The user can however configure his computer as follows, to refuse the installation of cookies:

- **Under Internet Explorer**: tool tab (cog-shaped pictogram in the upper right) / internet options. Click on Confidentiality and choose Block all cookies. Validate on Ok.
- **In Firefox**: at the top of the browser window, click on the Firefox button, then go to the Options tab. Click on the Privacy tab. Configure the Storage rules on: use personalized parameters for history. Finally, uncheck it to deactivate cookies.
- **In Safari**: Click on the menu icon at the top right of the browser (symbolized by a cog). Select Settings. Click on Show advanced settings. In the 'Confidentiality' section, click on Content settings. In the 'Cookies' section, you can block cookies.
- **Under Chrome**: Click on the menu icon at the top right of the browser (symbolized by three horizontal lines). Select Settings. Click on Show advanced settings. In the 'Confidentiality' section, click on preferences. In the 'Confidentiality' tab, you can block cookies.

**Note**: Our website analytics (Plausible) does not use cookies. Any cookies that may be set are for functional purposes only (such as theme preferences stored in your browser's local storage).
